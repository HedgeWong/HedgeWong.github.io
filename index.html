I am a first-year Ph.D. student at Georgia Tech, advised by Prof. Arijit Raychowdhury at ICSRL. I am particularly interested in computer architecture, VLSI design, and robotics.

Before coming to Georgia Tech, I received my M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Harvard University in 2020. During my master, I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Vijay Janapa Reddi at Edge Computing Lab, and collaborated with Prof. David Brooks and Prof. Gu-Yeon Wei at VLSI-Arch Lab.

I received my B.Eng. in Electrical Engineering from Harbin Institute of Technology in 2018. During my undergrad, I was fortunate to work with Prof. Dianguo Xu and Prof. Xueguang Zhang.

I was a cross-registered student at MIT from 2018 to 2020, and a visiting student at National Chiao-Tung University and National Tsing-Hua University in 2017.

Selected Course Projects

I'm interested in hardware-software co-design for AI applications, and I've worked on projects spanning all layers of computing stack, from higher-level algorithm/application, runtime systems to lower-level computer architecture, circuits. Please take a look at my projects below to know more.

AutoX: Automating Algorithm-SoC Co-design for Aerial Robots
Instructor: Prof. Vijay Janapa Reddi.
Course: Edge Computing: Autonomous Machines   (Best Project Award)
(Under Paper Submission)

We proposed an agile HW-SW co-design flow for aerial robots. The front-end is to design and validate end-to-end models for robot tasks, and the back-end is to synthesize hardware accelerators for processing the model efficiently based on the HLS approach.
SoC-DNN Design Space Exploration and Optimization
Instructor: Prof. David Brooks.
Course: Advanced Computer Architecture
(Under Paper Submission)

We implemented an systolic array-based AutoDSE framework to study the effect of memory, dataflow and array size acros NN topologies, and proposed a per-layer frequency scaling mathematical model.
Image Pre-processor for Robust DNN Resistant to Adversarial Attacks
Instructor: Prof. Vivienne Sze.
Course: Design and Analysis of Digital Integrated Circuits   (Best Project Award)
Project Report / Slide

We proposed and designed an image pre-processor with three different image filtering algorithms for robust DNN resistant to adversarial attacks. Performed RTL synthesis, layout, place and route using Cadence 90nm technology.
Study of Posit Numeric in Speech Recognition Neural Inference
Instructor: Prof. David Brooks.
Course: Advanced Topics in Computer Architecture
Project Report / Slide

We studied the impact of various precisions and datatypes on speech-to-text neural inference, with an emphasize on Posit.
MIPS Processor Implementation and Software Optimization of Matrix Multiplication
